Your portfolio is carefully constructed based on the account type you select. Use IRAs to save on taxes while saving for retirement. Use taxable accounts to enjoy unrestricted access to your investments. Our portfolio management systems work best when your accounts are consolidated in one place.

Taxable Accounts

Individual Accounts
Our most common account type, these accounts are designed for individuals looking to build general purpose wealth. Unlike retirement accounts, individual accounts are taxable.
Joint Accounts
These accounts are similar to our individual accounts but allow two or more people to share account privileges and are typically used by married couples, close relatives or business partners.
Trust Accounts
These accounts are useful for the eventual transfer or handing down of assets according to your instructions.

Retirement Accounts

Traditional IRAs
Contributions to these accounts are tax-deductible within certain income limits and taxes are not due until withdrawal.
401(k) Rollovers
Consolidate your past employer 401(k) accounts into a single IRA. Current employer 401(k) contributions are not eligible to be rolled over until you leave your employer.
Roth IRAs
Contributions to these accounts grow tax-free if withdrawn during retirement or for qualified purposes.
SEP IRAs
These accounts allows small businesses to offer retirement plans for their employees or themselves.

Tax-Efficient Portfolio Management

HATS is our automated tax-management system and is designed to ensure you pay no more in taxes than are necessary. HATS was conceived with four basic principles in mind:

Harvesting Of Losses

A commonly used tax management strategy is to sell your losing assets in order to receive a tax deduction and immediately rebuy a similar asset so that you don’t miss out on potential gains going forward. We analyze your portfolio daily for the opportunity to realize losses which can be used to reduce your income tax liability.

Automated Rebalancing

As your assets change in value, your portfolio may shift from the allocations we initially recommend. When this happens, we rebalance your portfolio. We also use your cash flows from contributions, withdrawals and dividends to keep your portfolio in balance and minimize taxes on asset sales.

Tax-Efficient Asset Location

We’ll place taxable bonds in your tax-free and tax-different accounts and municipal bonds in your taxable accounts where they can both grow tax free.

Specific Identification Lot Liquidation

Specific Identification Lot Liquidation means that during rebalancing events and withdrawals, we’ll first liquidate those shares with the lowest estimated tax-impact ensuring you pay the lowest possible capital gains taxes on asset sales.
